While individual underwriting offers granular control, it requires active management of risk points, mutex groups, and leverage ratios. For users who prefer a "set-and-forget" experience, LayerCover offers **Syndicates**.


## What is a Syndicate?

A Syndicate is a specialized, manager-led vault (ERC-4626) that aggregates capital from multiple depositors to deploy liquidity across LayerCover's risk pools.

It acts as a bridge between passive capital and active risk management:
* **You (The Depositor):** Deposit a single asset (e.g., USDC) and receive Syndicate Shares representing your pro-rata ownership of the vault.
* **The Manager:** A professional risk assessor or DAO who actively manages the vault's allocations, diversification, and leverage strategies.

## How It Works

The architecture decouples the complexity of allocation from the provision of liquidity.

1.  **Aggregation:** Users deposit capital into the Syndicate Vault.
2.  **Delegation:** The Syndicate Manager holds the exclusive authority to allocate this pooled capital via the core Underwriter Manager.
3.  **Deployment:** The Manager selects specific Risk Pools, adjusts leverage, and ensures compliance with Mutex Groups (to avoid correlated risks).
4.  **Yield:** Premiums and rewards earned by these allocations flow back into the Syndicate Vault, increasing the value of your shares.

### Dual-Strategy Deployment

Syndicate Managers are not limited to passive pools. They can utilize the **Fixed Rate (Intent)** market to generate alpha:

* **Variable Allocation:** The Manager allocates capital to standard risk pools to earn passive streaming premiums.
* **Fixed Rate Market Making:** The Manager can sign cryptographic "Intents" to underwrite specific large-scale policies for a fixed term and fee. This allows the Syndicate to capture higher premiums from institutional buyers who demand price certainty.

## Key Benefits

### For Depositors (Passive)
* **Simplicity:** No need to analyze individual protocols, manage risk points, or monitor daily solvency. You simply hold the vault token.
* **Expertise:** Benefit from the specialized risk assessment strategies of professional managers.
* **Gas Efficiency:** Pooling capital reduces the transaction costs associated with rebalancing a portfolio of many small positions.

### For Managers (Active)
* **AUM Scaling:** Managers can monetize their risk expertise by deploying a large capital base without needing to self-fund the entire principal.
* **Revenue Stream:** Managers earn performance and management fees, creating a sustainable business model for risk assessment.

## Fee Structure & Incentives

To align interests, Syndicates utilize a configurable, on-chain fee model. Fees are accrued automatically and claimed by the Manager:

* **Performance Fees:** Calculated using a **High-Watermark** formula based on the vault's share value appreciation (e.g., net profit from premiums). This ensures managers are only paid when they generate real value.
* **Management Fees:** A streaming rate charged on the total assets under management (AUM).

<Callout type="info">
  **Protection:** All fees are capped at the factory level (e.g., max 20%) to protect depositors from excessive extraction.
</Callout>

## Withdrawals and Liquidity

Syndicate shares function similarly to the underlying capital pools regarding solvency and timing.

* **Notice Period:** Withdrawals follow a governance-defined **30-day notice period**. This prevents "hot money" from entering to farm yield and leaving immediately before a claim, protecting the long-term solvency of the coverage.
* **Redemption:** Once the notice period elapses, you redeem your shares for the underlying asset (e.g., USDC) at the current Price-Per-Share.
* **Solvency Check:** If the Syndicate is heavily allocated to pools that are currently paying out claims, withdrawals may be paused or rate-limited until solvency floors are met.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How fast can I exit a syndicate?**
You must initiate a withdrawal request, which triggers the **30-day notice period**. Note that if the Manager has locked capital in **Fixed Rate Intents** (which have a fixed duration, e.g., 90 days), that specific portion of liquidity may not be withdrawable until the active policies expire.

**What happens if the manager stops engaging?**
LPs can still redeem after applicable notice periods even if allocations are stale. The underlying capital remains in the vault and obeys protocol-level solvency rules.

**Can managers withdraw my funds?**
No. They can allocate/deallocate and claim performance fees, but they cannot rug-pull LP principal.

**Do syndicates pay the same protocol fees as direct underwriters?**
Yes. Syndicates are just "managed" underwriters; they pay the same Reinsurance Tax and claim fees.

**Is leverage guaranteed to improve returns?**
No. Leverage amplifies both upside and downside. Managers use Mutex Groups to manage this, but losses can still exceed premiums if risk is misjudged.
